An edublog is a blog created for educational purposes. Edublogs a rchive and support student and teacher learning by facilitating reflection, questioning by self an d others, collaboration[1] and by providing contexts for engaging in higher-order thinking.[2][3] Edu blogs proliferated when blogging architecture became more simplified and teachers perceived the instructional potenti al of blogs as an online resource.[1] The use of blogs has become popular in education institutions including public schools an d colleges.[4] Blogs can be useful tools for sharing information and tips among co-workers, providing information for students, or keeping in contact with parents. Common examples include blogs written by or for teache rs, blogs maintained for the purpose of classroom instruction, or blogs written about educational policy. Educators who blog are sometimes called edubloggers.
